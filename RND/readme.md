# Research & Development Head

## Task

### **Design Two Semester-Long Technical Project Proposals**

Develop **two semester-long technical project proposals** for **any two** of the following R&D committees:

* AI/ML
* Systems & Security (SysCom)
* Web Development
* Game Development

If your idea doesn't fit under any of these umbrellas, feel free to step outside the box. (e.g. something in Theoretical Computer Science, Computational Science, etc., that's still computer science which will fall under the R&D committee)

The objective is to evaluate your ability to identify meaningful technical opportunities, plan long-term projects, organize teams, and create a strong learning environment within the club.

Your projects should be technically engaging, feasible for undergraduate students, and encourage collaboration, learning, and innovation.

---

## Submission Requirements

For **each** project proposal, include:

### 1. Project Overview

* Problem statement
* Objectives
* Expected impact
* Why the project is worth pursuing

### 2. Technical Plan

* Proposed solution
* Technologies and tools
* Expected challenges
* High-level project roadmap with key milestones

### 3. Team & Learning Plan

Assume a team of **8–10 members**.

Briefly describe:

* Team structure and task delegation
* How you would onboard and mentor beginners
* Resources or workshops required throughout the project

### 4. Success Metrics

Explain how you would measure the success of the project.

Examples include:

* Project completion
* Member participation
* Documentation quality
* Technical innovation
* Community impact

---

## Key Deliverables

The primary deliverable is a **Proof of Concept (PoC)** for **one** of your proposed projects. The PoC should focus on the project's design and feasibility rather than implementation.

Your submission should include:

* A project proposal document covering the required sections
* A **high-level system architecture** (diagram or flowchart)
* An explanation of the project's components, workflows, and technical decisions
* A proposed implementation roadmap outlining how the PoC could evolve into a semester-long project
* A **5–10 minute technical presentation or demo video** explaining the project, its architecture, your research process, and how you would lead its development

> **Note:** A working implementation or source code is **not required**. The emphasis is on research, planning, system design, and technical reasoning.

---

## Success Criteria

Evaluation will be based on:

* Technical understanding
* Quality and feasibility of the proposed architecture
* Project planning
* Leadership and organization
* Originality
* Documentation and presentation

---

## Presentation

Prepare a **10–15 minute presentation** explaining your two project proposals, the architecture of your selected PoC, the reasoning behind your technical decisions, and how you would lead the project from ideation to completion.
